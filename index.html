Important Note

Sharia Law Principles

1. Religious Practices (Ibadat)

  • Mandatory daily prayers (Salah).
  • Fasting during Ramadan (Sawm).
  • Obligatory almsgiving (Zakat).
  • Pilgrimage to Mecca (Hajj) if physically and financially able.

2. Interpersonal Relations (Muamalat)

  • Marriage contracts and family rights.
  • Inheritance laws specifying shares for family members.
  • Prohibition of interest (Riba) in financial dealings.
  • Requirement of honesty and fairness in trade.

3. Criminal Law (Hudud, Qisas, and Ta’zir)

  • Hudud:
    • Theft: Punishable by amputation of the hand under strict conditions.
    • Adultery: Chastisement or stoning depending on marital status.
    • Alcohol Consumption: Punishable by flogging.
    • False Accusation: Punishable by flogging without evidence.
    • Highway Robbery: Severe punishments, including death or mutilation.
  • Qisas: Retributive justice, allowing the victim or their family to seek equal punishment or compensation (diya).
  • Ta’zir: Discretionary punishments determined by judges for cases not covered under Hudud or Qisas.

4. Moral and Ethical Principles

  • Modesty in dress and behavior for men and women.
  • Prohibition of gambling and intoxicants.
  • Dietary restrictions, including the prohibition of pork and alcohol.

5. Apostasy (Riddah)

  • Leaving Islam may be punishable by death in some interpretations.

6. Family Law

  • Marriage as a legal and religious contract.
  • Polygamy allowed up to four wives with conditions of fairness.
  • Divorce regulations for both men and women.

7. Warfare and Peace (Jihad)

  • Jihad can signify spiritual struggle or defensive war.
